INTRODUCTION

MS Dhoni is a former Indian cricketer and one of the most successful captains in Indian cricket history. Known as "Captain Cool," he led the Indian cricket team to victory in major tournaments like the T20 World Cup, Cricket World Cup, and Champions Trophy. Dhoni was admired for his calm demeanor, innovative batting, and exceptional wicketkeeping skills. He holds several records and is considered one of the greatest wicketkeeper-batsmen and finishers in the game. Dhoni retired from international cricket in 2020, leaving a lasting legacy as an inspirational leader and a beloved figure in Indian cricket.

Career Highlights

1998:

Dhoni was selected by Deval Sahay to play for the Central Coal Fields Limited (CCL) team. He performed exceptionally well at CCL, batting higher up the order. Impressed by his performance, Deval Sahay pushed for his selection in the Bihar team. Dhoni moved to the Ranchi team and eventually the senior Bihar Ranji Team.

1998-99:

Dhoni played for the U-19 Bihar team in the Cooch Behar Trophy. Scored 176 runs in 5 matches but Bihar did not qualify for the quarter-finals. Not selected for the East Zone U-19 squad or Rest of India squad.

1999-2000:

Dhoni played for the Bihar U-19 team in the Cooch Behar Trophy. Scored 84 in the final, helping Bihar post a total of 357 runs. Bihar lost the final as Punjab scored 839 runs. Dhoni had an impressive overall performance with 488 runs, 5 fifties, 17 catches, and 7 stumpings. Selected for the East Zone U-19 squad in the CK Nayudu Trophy but scored only 97 runs in four matches. East Zone team finished last in the tournament.

2000-2001:

Dhoni scored his maiden first-class century while playing for Bihar against Bengal. Apart from the century, his performance in the season did not include any other score over fifty.

2001-2002:

Dhoni scored five fifties in four Ranji matches.

2002-03:

Dhoni scored three half-centuries in the Ranji Trophy. He also scored a couple of half-centuries in the Deodhar Trophy. Dhoni started gaining recognition for his lower-order contribution and hard-hitting batting style.

2003/04 Season:

Dhoni scored a century (128*) against Assam in the first match of the Ranji ODI tournament. He was part of the East Zone squad that won the Deodhar Trophy. Dhoni contributed with 244 runs in 4 matches, including a century (114) against Central zone. He was picked over Deep Dasgupta to represent East Zone in the Duleep Trophy finals. Dhoni scored a fighting half-century in the second innings but ended up losing the match.

India A Team:

Dhoni was picked for the India A squad for a tour of Zimbabwe and Kenya. He had his best wicket-keeping effort with 7 catches and 4 stumpings in a match against the Zimbabwe XI. Dhoni helped India A chase down their target of 223 against Pakistan A with a half-century. He scored back-to-back centuries (120 and 119*) against Pakistan A. Dhoni scored 362 runs in 6 innings at an average of 72.40.

International Career:

Dhoni made his ODI debut on the Bangladesh tour in 2004/05 but got run out for a duck. He scored his first ODI century (148) against Pakistan in his fifth match. Dhoni scored an unbeaten 183 off 145 balls against Sri Lanka in 2005, helping India win the game. He ended the series with the highest run aggregate (346) and was awarded the Man of the Series. Dhoni overtook Ricky Ponting as the number one batsman in the ICC ODI Rankings. He was named in the World ODI XI by the ICC for his performances in 2006. Dhoni had a disappointing 2007 World Cup, with India crashing out in the group stage. He scored 91* against Bangladesh after India's early wickets fell. Dhoni was named vice-captain of the ODI team for the series against South Africa and England.
